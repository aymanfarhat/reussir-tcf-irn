## Expression orale  - 10 minutes, 3 parts, no preparation, recorded

Structure of the oral exam in French:

### Take 1: Présentation / questions personnelles (Directed interview / personal questions), 2 to 3 minutes
    “Parlez-moi de vous”, “Pourquoi apprenez-vous le français ?”, “Que faites-vous dans la vie ?”

### Task 2: Interaction situationnelle (Interaction / role play / asking or giving information), 3 to 4 minutes
    You need to ask questions, clarify, react, negotiate.
    Example: calling a school, asking about a course, choosing an activity, asking for information.

### Task 3: Opinion / point de vue (Expressing and defending an opinion), 3 to 4 minutes
    This is the most important for B2.
    Example: “Le télétravail est-il une bonne chose ?”, “Faut-il limiter les voitures en ville ?”


## Expression écrite (30 minutes, 3 tasks)

For TCF IRN, the written tasks are much shorter than normal TCF Tout Public. A current preparation source summarizing the IRN format gives this practical breakdown:

### Task 1 (7 mins)  - Short descriptive message (30–60 words)

### Task 2 (9 mins) - Short narrative / daily-life experience (40-90 words)

### Task 3 (12 mins) - Short opinion text (40-90 words)

Remaining time to review and edit your work.

## Notes

For TCF IRN, do not train mainly on long 150–180 word essays. That is more relevant to other TCF versions. Focus is “Write a clear short message, short story, or short opinion with correct structure, good connectors, and few blocking mistakes.”
